## Attention!

You must have approval before using video in your hero area. Contact your web
team to learn more.

Video can be difficult to work with, and creating a good background video for
a hero section is tricky. To be approved for using video in your hero area,
you must agree to adhere to the standards below. If you need help finding or
producing video content, contact your creative/web services team.

## Standards

### HD quality

Use video that is 720p. You may use a higher-quality clip if it is very short
(less than 3 seconds). Remember to keep the file size as small as possible.

### Try not to be distracting

Video with fast-moving content or jarring edits can distract from the page
and, in some cases, even make users feel nauseous. Keep content and movement
in mind when selecting a video. Clips with subtle movement, slow motion, or
timelapses are a great way to enhance a page.

### Keep it short

Edit video down to under 10 seconds.

### Darker video works better

By default, hero videos are darkened 35%. When using a brighter video, you
may want to edit it to appear darker so that text displayed over it stays
legible.

### Remove audio

Remove any audio from your video to reduce file size.

### Save as .mp4

Use the MP4 file type when exporting or saving.

### Compress

Use a compressor to reduce the file size of your video. Try to keep files as
small as possible — under 5MB — since smaller files load faster. [Handbrake](https://handbrake.fr/)
is a free video compression application.

### Loop

Hero videos autoplay on a loop. Use a video editor to trim the beginning and
end of the video, or duplicate and reverse the playback, to create a seamless
loop.

### Fallback image

Recommended size: 1280x720. Video does not display on touch or mobile
devices — a fallback image is used instead. Use a screenshot of the video's
first frame, or similar content, to prevent a jump when the video is loading
on slower connections.

### Optimize for web

Hero video should be optimized to load quickly for seamless playback. This
helps prevent a delay when users first load a page containing the hero
video. Video editors such as Handbrake can export your MP4 using the "Web
Optimized" setting — see Handbrake's documentation on web optimization for
more detail.
