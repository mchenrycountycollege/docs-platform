Maria Elena Vásquez

Fragmentos del Tiempo

  • January 15 — March 28, 2026
  • Artist Talk: February 10, 2026 at 6:00 PM
  • Reception: January 15, 2026 at 5:00–7:00 PM

Untitled No. 7  ·  Oil on canvas  ·  2025

My work investigates the tension between memory and forgetting — how fragments of the past persist in material form even as their meaning dissolves. The paintings in Fragmentos del Tiempo were made over three years in a studio overlooking the coastline of Oaxaca, where the light changes so quickly that any fixed image is immediately a kind of lie.

I work in oil because oil resists. It stays malleable long enough to think inside it. These are not documents of landscape; they are landscapes of a searching mind.

Maria Elena Vásquez (b. 1977, Mexico City) is a painter and installation artist whose work has been exhibited throughout Mexico and the United States. She received her MFA from the School of the Art Institute of Chicago and has held residencies at the MacDowell Colony, the Headlands Center for the Arts, and the Bellagio Center.

Her work is in the permanent collections of the Museum of Contemporary Art Chicago and the Smithsonian American Art Museum. She lives and works in Chicago, Illinois.

James Kowalski

Structural Residue

  • February 2 — April 4, 2026

James Kowalski is a sculptor and visual artist based in Elgin, Illinois. His practice centers on the transformation of industrial and reclaimed materials, exploring themes of labor, time, and built environments. He earned his BFA from Northern Illinois University and his MFA from the University of Illinois at Chicago.

Kowalski has exhibited at venues throughout the Chicago area and has completed public art commissions for the cities of Elgin and Waukegan. Structural Residue is his first solo exhibition at McHenry County College.
